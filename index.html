Projects
01

Marketing Campaign ROI Analysis

SQL PostgreSQL Power BI
View on GitHub →

Analyzed 12 marketing campaigns across email, Google, Meta, and SEO channels. Built SQL queries to calculate ROI, budget utilization, monthly revenue trends, and revenue share by campaign — then visualized results in Power BI.

Key Findings
  • Email campaigns dominated — Promo Blast, Re-engagement, and Welcome Series returned ROIs of 2,747%, 2,655%, and 2,605% respectively, driven by low cost and warm audiences.
  • All three Meta campaigns produced negative ROI (–59% to –70%), with spend far exceeding returns.
  • Every campaign ran over budget — a pattern worth flagging for finance and future planning.
  • Revenue spiked in April and August due to overlapping campaign windows, making channel attribution difficult to isolate.
02

Grocery Store Sales Analysis

SQL PostgreSQL Power BI
View on GitHub →

Explored a multi-table e-commerce dataset to surface top products, monthly revenue trends, and high-value customers. Used multi-table joins, aggregations, and date truncation across three focused queries, then built accompanying Power BI dashboards.

Key Findings
  • Top products by revenue were tightly clustered between $18.2M–$18.9M — no single runaway winner, suggesting a healthy, distributed product mix.
  • Revenue dropped sharply in May 2018 to ~$300M vs. a ~$1B monthly average — likely a data artifact worth investigating.
  • Top customer (Lesley Perry, $263K) spent ~12% more than the second-highest — a notable gap worth exploring for retention strategy.
